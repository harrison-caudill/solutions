\begin{question}

  A two-state system is characterized by the Hamiltonian

  \begin{equation*}
    H = H_{11} \dyad{1} + H_{22} \dyad{2} + H_{12}[\dyad{1}{2} + \dyad{2}{1}]
  \end{equation*}
  
  where $H_{11}$, $H_{22}$, and $H_{12}$ are real numbers with the
  dimension of energy, and $\ket{1}$ and $\ket{2}$ are eigenkets of
  some observable ($\ne H$). Find the energy eigenkets and
  corresponding energy eigenvalues. Make sure that your answer makes
  good sense for $H_{12} = 0$.

\end{question}

\begin{purpose}
  There are two main purposes to this problem:
  \begin{enumerate}

  \item Repetition with the mechanics of eigenvalue problems.

  \item Connecting the eigenvalue problems to hamiltonians and state
    energies.
  \end{enumerate}
\end{purpose}

\begin{answer}

  Let's put the basic matrix representation together:

  \begin{equation}
    H \dot{=}
    \begin{bmatrix}
      H_{11} & H_{12}\\
      H_{12} & H_{22}\\
    \end{bmatrix}
  \end{equation}

  Putting together our characteristic equation

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{split}
      0 &=
      det\left(
      \begin{bmatrix}
        H_{11} - \lambda & H_{12}\\
        H_{12} & H_{22} - \lambda\\
      \end{bmatrix}
      \right)\\
      &= (H_{11} - \lambda)(H_{22} - \lambda) - H_{12}^2\\
      &= H_{11}H_{22} - H_{11}\lambda - H_{22}\lambda + \lambda^2 - H_{12}^2\\
      &= \lambda^2 - (H_{11} + H_{22})\lambda + H_{11}H_{22} - H_{12}^2\\
    \end{split}
  \end{equation}

  Since the problem discusses it, we should probably point out that if
  $H_{12} = 0$ then it's already factored and $\lambda_{1,2} =
  H_{11,22}$ and the Eigenbasis is shared as the eigenvectors become
  $\begin{bmatrix}1\\0\end{bmatrix}$ and
    $\begin{bmatrix}0\\1\end{bmatrix}$.

  In general, however, we'll want to solve for $\lambda$.  Pulling out
  the Po-Shen Loh
  method \footnote{https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XKBX0r3J-9Y}:

  \begin{equation}
    \lambda_{1,2} = -\frac{B}{2} \pm \sqrt{\frac{B^2}{4} - C}
  \end{equation}

  where $B = (H_{11} + H_{22})$ and $C = H_{11}H_{22} - H_{12}^2$
  making

  \begin{equation}
    \inlabel{theBigUgly}
    \begin{split}
      \lambda_{1,2} &= \frac{(H_{11} + H_{22})}{2} \pm
      \sqrt{\frac{(H_{11} + H_{22})^2}{4} - H_{11}H_{22} + H_{12}^2}\\
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22} \pm
      \sqrt{(H_{11} + H_{22})^2 - 4H_{11}H_{22} + 4H_{12}^2}\right)\\
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22}\pm
      \sqrt{H_{11}^2 + 2H_{11}H_{22} + H_{22}^2 - 4H_{11}H_{22} + 4H_{12}^2}\right)\\
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22}) \pm
      \sqrt{H_{11}^2 - 2H_{11}H_{22} + H_{22}^2 + 4H_{12}^2}\right)\\
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22} \pm
      \sqrt{(H_{11} - H_{22})^2 + 4H_{12}^2}\right)\\
    \end{split}
  \end{equation}

  We can see that if we set $H_{12} = 0$ then we find our Eigenvalues
  to be:

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{split}
      \lambda_{1,2}
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22} \pm
      \sqrt{(H_{11} - H_{22})^2 + 4H_{12}^2}\right)\\
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22} \pm \sqrt{(H_{11} - H_{22})^2}\right)\\
      &= \frac{1}{2}\left(H_{11} + H_{22} \pm (H_{11} - H_{22})\right)\\
    \end{split}
  \end{equation}

  In the general case, however, we have to go back to equation
  \inref{theBigUgly}, albeit with a few simplifying assertions:

  \begin{equation}
    u \equiv \sqrt{(H_{11} - H_{22})^2 + 4H_{12}^2}
  \end{equation}

  That transforms equation \inref{theBigUgly} into

  \begin{equation}
    \lambda_{1,2} = \frac 1 2 (H_{11} + H_{22} \pm u)
  \end{equation}

  Solving for our first Eigenvector, we'll start by setting our
  non-normalized $x_1 = 1$:

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{alignedat}{2}
      & & H_{11}x_1 + H_{12}y_1 = \lambda_1x_1\\
      \implies \quad & & H_{11} + H_{12}y_1 = \lambda_1\\
      \implies \quad & & y_1 = \frac{\lambda_1 - H_{11}}{H_{12}}\\
      \implies \quad & & y_1 = \frac{1}{2}\frac{H_{22} - H_{11}+
      \sqrt{(H_{11} - H_{22})^2 + 4H_{12}^2}}{H_{12}}\\
    \end{alignedat}
  \end{equation}

  Similarly, setting $y_2 = 1$ we find that
  
  \begin{equation}
    \begin{alignedat}{2}
      & & H_{12}x_2 + H_{22}y_2 = \lambda_2y_2\\
      \implies \quad & & x_2 = \frac{\lambda_2 - H_{22}}{H_{12}}\\
      \implies \quad & & x_2 = \frac{1}{2}\frac{H_{11} - H_{22} -
      \sqrt{(H_{11} - H_{22})^2 + 4H_{12}^2}}{H_{12}}\\
    \end{alignedat}
  \end{equation}

  If you want to find the normalization constants for that...be my
  guest.

  It's worth noting that you can use mathematics discussed in later
  chapters to reduce the problem further by incorporating angles
  between elements of the matrix.  Since the formalism of angles
  between components of a generalized 2D Hilbert space is well beyond
  the scope of chapter 1, however, that discussion is \emph{not}
  included here.

\end{answer}
