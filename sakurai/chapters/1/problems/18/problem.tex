\begin{question}

  Two Hermitian operators anticommute:
  \begin{equation*}
    \{A, B\} = AB + BA = 0
  \end{equation*}

  Is it possible to have a simultaneous (that is, common) eigenket of
  $A$ and $B$? Prove or illustrate your assertion.

\end{question}

\begin{purpose}
  Developing an intuition about degeneracy is pretty important to
  Sakurai and this is another stepping-stone along that path.
\end{purpose}

\begin{answer}

  Drawing upon equation \outref{1}{17}{result}, we know that if the
  eigenbasis is fully-shared and non-degenerate then they commute (and
  hence an't anti-commute).  However, the problem (so I just realized
  only after completing literally every other problem in the chapter)
  asks about a \emph{single} shared eigenket.

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{split}
      AB
      &=
      \left(\sum_{i} a_i\Lambda_{a,i}\right)
      \left(\sum_{j} b_j\Lambda_{b,j}\right)\\
    \end{split}
  \end{equation}

  If we assume a single shared eigenket, then

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{split}
      0
      &= AB + BA\\
      &=
      \left(a_s\Lambda_s + \sum_{i} a_i\Lambda_{a,i}\right)
      \left(b_s\Lambda_s + \sum_{j} b_j\Lambda_{b,j}\right)
      +
      \left(b_s\Lambda_s + \sum_{j} b_j\Lambda_{b,j}\right)
      \left(a_s\Lambda_s + \sum_{i} a_i\Lambda_{a,i}\right)
      \\
    \end{split}
  \end{equation}

  Really all we need to show is what those $a_s$ and $b_s$ and
  $\Lambda_s$ values are up to since we can safely assume that the
  rest take care of themselves.  We know that the shared basis is
  orthogonal to the other bases, which means that we also know that

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{alignedat}{2}
      &&0
      &= AB + BA\\
      &&&= a_sb_s\Lambda_s + b_sa_s\Lambda_s + \ldots\\
      &&&= 2a_sb_s\Lambda_s + \ldots\\
      \implies && a_sb_s = 0\\
    \end{alignedat}
  \end{equation}

  So, for our shared eigenket to be consistent with anticommutation,
  clearly one (or both) of the eigenvalues for that shared ket will
  need to be $0$.

\end{answer}
