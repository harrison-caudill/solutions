\begin{question}

  Two Hermitian operators anticommute:
  \begin{equation*}
    \{A, B\} = AB + BA = 0
  \end{equation*}

  Is it possible to have a simultaneous (that is, common) eigenket of
  $A$ and $B$? Prove or illustrate your assertion.

\end{question}

\begin{purpose}
  The purpose of this one is a little unclear.  He's either checking
  that we understand the basics of how to eigen-decompose an operator
  or...I don't know...maybe he's building to something.
\end{purpose}

\begin{answer}

  Let's start by decomposing the operator into its eigenvectors.
  
  Drawing upon equation \outref{1}{17}{result}, we see that whenever
  there \emph{is} a simultaneous Eigenbasis, then

  \begin{equation}
    AB = BA
  \end{equation}

  So, if we apply that here, we would have:

  \begin{equation}
    \begin{split}
      0
      &= AB + BA\\
      &= AB + AB\\
      &= 2AB\\
    \end{split}
  \end{equation}

  For this equation to hold, either $A$ or $B$ (or both) must be $0$.

\end{answer}
