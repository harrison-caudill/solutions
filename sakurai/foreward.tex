\chapter{Foreward to the First Edition}
  A lot of what we're doing here depends upon trade-skill familiarity
  with a bunch of these tools.  It's unresonable to ask people to
  re-derive the chain-rule, for example, so execution depends upon
  simply being accustomed to reaching for these tools.  As I went
  through this exploration, I relied quite heavily upon external
  sources, and found that the simple practice of using those tools
  bred the familiarity I sought.  I don't think it would be a
  productive use of my time to go searching every time -- not when
  reasonable alternatives exist.  In prior years, these modern tools
  did not exist and so it was, not so much reasonable, as necessary
  that one came into this topic with high levels of skill in things
  like taking integrals.  Now, however, we get to fill out those
  capabilities in a much more targeted way letting reality be our
  guide all by employing one thing: the modern search engine.  The
  ability of modern LLMs (using some crazy dark-wizardy combination of
  Markov processes and multi-billion dminesional vector spaces) to map
  a problem to the most-recognized correct approach makes the
  experience much more productive -- you don't learn physics by
  banging your head against an integral that everyone else has already
  solved because they know ``the trick''.

  That concept of ``trade skill'' is incredibly important, and
  something I did not understand in undergrad.  In this companion
  guide to (the first chapter of) Sakurai's book, you will the
  concepts of trade school come through.  The primary objective is to
  establish skill of execution with sufficient theoretical knowledge
  to underpin those skills.  This shouldn't be rote regurgitation, but
  we're not trying to unlock the secrets of the universe either (well,
  except in problem \ref{1.24:theQuestion} where I go a little
  overboard).  I try to call out connections to other concepts and
  tools as I go to help make connections, but mostly I focus on
  practicality and skill of execution.
  
  I don't call out the places where I relied upon an LLM to show me
  that trick, but I definitely note the change in how I approach much
  of the math in the chapter and how that has evolved over the course
  of writing this guide.
